    Hello World

    Posted on 2025-10-22 Edited on 2025-11-06 Views:

    Hello, World!

    It’s the first output every programmer writes—and I’ll use this as the first post of my blog. They both start from 0 to 1.
    I created this blog the day before my birthday, and in that moment, I gets a writing space truly my own.

    Purpose

    Writing excites me. Even if no one reads my work, I still feel proud of what I create. It’s like the excitement I felt when I wrote my first line of code and saw the expected result on screen—sharing through writing gives me that same sense of “achievement.” It organizes my disordered thoughts, just like tidying up a cluttered desk.
    Surely, sharing that joy with others would make it even better. I guess that comes from the “open-source spirit” comes from every programmer. Throughout my coding journey, I’ve benefited so much from the experiences of those who came before me. Their detailed troubleshooting notes and clear programming tutorials helped me overcome countless obstacles and fix one error after another. Now, I want to be part of this community. Even offering a tiny perspective would make it all worthwhile.
    No matter what, I hope this blog becomes a part of my life. I’m happy to share about technology, entertainment, or random thoughts—anything that matters to me.
    In this digital era, the open-source spirit and knowledge sharing drive the progress of online communities. I don’t want to just be a consumer of information; I want to be a creator. I want to use words to connect with the world.

    Blog Tech Stack

    This blog is built with the Hexo Next theme. The Chinese version is hosted on a Yuyun server at 154.44.8.95, and the English version is on tev6.github.io via GitHub Pages.
    I chose the Next theme because it balances simplicity with visual appeal and clarity. I didn’t like unnecessary interfaces or features cluttering the blog—sharing information is my top priority. The theme’s minimalist design helps readers focus without distractions.

    Closing Thoughts

    In the future, more content will gradually accumulate here. There might be unpolished views or technical oversights, but every post will be the product of deep contemplation.
